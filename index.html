🔭 Physics – Ray Optics & Optical Instruments ~4–5 marks
MUST
Mirror Formula & Magnification
1/f = 1/v + 1/u. Sign convention critical. Numericals every year — concave/convex mirrors.
MUST
Refraction & Snell's Law
n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂. Apparent depth = real depth/n. Lateral shift in glass slab.
MUST
Total Internal Reflection & Critical Angle
sinC = 1/n. Applications: optical fibre, mirage, diamond sparkle. Direct MCQ every year.
MUST
Lens Formula & Lens Maker's Equation
1/f = 1/v − 1/u. Lens maker's: 1/f = (n−1)[1/R₁−1/R₂]. Power P = 1/f(m). Combination of lenses.
MUST
Prism – Deviation & Minimum Deviation
δ = (n−1)A for thin prism. At min deviation: r = A/2. n = sin[(A+δm)/2]/sin(A/2). Frequently asked.
IMP
Microscope – Magnifying Power
Simple microscope: m = 1 + D/f. Compound: m = (L/f_o)×(D/f_e). Final image at infinity vs near point.
IMP
Telescope – Magnifying Power
Astronomical: m = f_o/f_e (normal adjustment). Length = f_o + f_e. Resolving power ∝ D.
IMP
Refraction at Spherical Surfaces
n₂/v − n₁/u = (n₂−n₁)/R. Used to derive lens maker's equation. Direct question possible.
IMP
Scattering of Light (Rayleigh)
Intensity ∝ 1/λ⁴. Sky is blue (shortest λ scattered most). Sun appears red at sunset. Clouds white.
MED
Eye Defects & Corrections
Myopia (concave lens), Hypermetropia (convex), Presbyopia, Astigmatism. Focal length calculation.
MED
Dispersion & Spectrum
Dispersive power ω = (μv−μr)/(μy−1). Angular dispersion = (μv−μr)A. Achromatic combination.
🔗 Chemistry – Coordination Compounds ~3–4 marks
MUST
IUPAC Nomenclature
Name ligands (anionic -o, neutral as is), metal with oxidation state, alphabetical ligand order. Direct MCQ every year.
MUST
Isomerism – Geometric (cis/trans)
Square planar & octahedral complexes. [MA₂B₂]: cis has both A adjacent. Very commonly asked.
MUST
Crystal Field Theory (CFT)
Octahedral: t₂g & eg splitting (Δo). Tetrahedral: Δt = 4/9Δo. Strong field/weak field ligands. CFSE calculation.
MUST
Magnetic Properties (Spin-only formula)
μ = √n(n+2) BM, n = unpaired electrons. High spin vs low spin configuration. Diamagnetic vs paramagnetic.
IMP
Werner's Theory & Basic Concepts
Primary vs secondary valence. Coordination number. Ionization sphere vs coordination sphere. EAN rule.
IMP
Structural Isomerism
Ionization isomerism, linkage isomerism (ambidentate ligands like NO₂⁻/ONO⁻), coordination isomerism, solvate isomerism.
IMP
Colour of Coordination Compounds
d-d transitions absorb certain λ, complementary colour observed. [Ti(H₂O)₆]³⁺ is purple. Spectrochemical series.
IMP
Valence Bond Theory (VBT)
sp³, dsp², d²sp³, sp³d² hybridization. Inner vs outer orbital complex. [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻ (dsp², square planar) vs [NiCl₄]²⁻ (sp³, tetrahedral).
MED
Stability of Complexes
Chelate effect, stability constant (Kf). Higher Kf = more stable. EDTA forms very stable complexes.
MED
Applications of Coordination Compounds
Cisplatin (cancer drug), chlorophyll (Mg), haemoglobin (Fe), vitamin B₁₂ (Co), EDTA in hardness removal.
📐 Mathematics – 3 Dimensional Geometry ~4 marks
MUST
Direction Cosines & Direction Ratios
l²+m²+n²=1. DC of line joining (x₁,y₁,z₁) and (x₂,y₂,z₂). Converting DR to DC. Condition for perpendicular lines: a₁a₂+b₁b₂+c₁c₂=0.
MUST
Equation of Line in 3D
Vector: r⃗ = a⃗ + λb⃗. Cartesian: (x−x₁)/a = (y−y₁)/b = (z−z₁)/c. Angle between two lines: cosθ = |a₁a₂+b₁b₂+c₁c₂|/(|b₁||b₂|).
MUST
Equation of Plane
Normal form: ax+by+cz=d. Through 3 points (determinant form). Intercept form: x/a+y/b+z/c=1. Angle between planes: cosθ = |a₁a₂+b₁b₂+c₁c₂|/(|n₁||n₂|).
MUST
Distance of Point from Plane
d = |ax₁+by₁+cz₁+d|/√(a²+b²+c²). Distance between parallel planes. Foot of perpendicular from point to plane.
IMP
Shortest Distance Between Skew Lines
SD = |(a₂−a₁)·(b₁×b₂)|/|b₁×b₂|. If SD = 0, lines are coplanar. Skew lines are neither parallel nor intersecting.
IMP
Angle Between Line and Plane
sinθ = |al+bm+cn|/√(a²+b²+c²). Line perpendicular to plane: direction ratios of line ∝ normal to plane. Line in plane: a·l+b·m+c·n = 0.
IMP
Coplanarity of Lines
Two lines are coplanar if (a₂−a₁)·(b₁×b₂) = 0. Used to check if lines intersect. Write as determinant condition.
MED
Section Formula & Midpoint in 3D
Point dividing P₁P₂ in ratio m:n: ((mx₂+nx₁)/(m+n), ...). Midpoint: average of coordinates. Centroid of triangle in 3D.
⚡ KCET Cheat Codes & Exam Strategy — Day 4
⚡ Physics Tricks
Mirror sign convention: All distances measured from pole. Distances in direction of incident ray = positive (+ve).
TIR trick: Critical angle is for denser to rarer medium. sinC = n_rare/n_dense.
Lens power: P = 1/f. Convex lens P > 0, concave P < 0. For combination: P = P₁ + P₂.
Prism at min deviation: Ray passes symmetrically. r₁ = r₂ = A/2. Always verify with this condition.
Telescope vs microscope: Telescope → large fₒ, small fₑ. Microscope → small fₒ, large fₑ (or choose same).
🔗 Chemistry Tricks
IUPAC order: Ligands alphabetically, then metal. Anionic complex gets -ate suffix (ferrate, cuprate, argentate).
Spectrochemical series: I⁻ < Br⁻ < S²⁻ < Cl⁻ < NO₂⁻ < F⁻ < OH⁻ < H₂O < NH₃ < en < CN⁻ < CO (weak → strong field).
High spin = weak field (large number of unpaired e⁻, paramagnetic). Low spin = strong field (fewer unpaired, may be diamagnetic).
Magnetic moment quick check: Fe³⁺ (d⁵, 5 unpaired): μ = √35 ≈ 5.92 BM. Cu²⁺ (d⁹, 1 unpaired): μ = √3 ≈ 1.73 BM.
📐 Maths Tricks
DC check: Always verify l²+m²+n²=1. If given DR, divide each by √(a²+b²+c²) to get DC.
Plane through 3 points: Use determinant |x−x₁, y−y₁, z−z₁; x₂−x₁, ...; x₃−x₁, ...| = 0.
Skew lines: Neither parallel (b₁ not ∥ b₂) nor intersecting. SD formula uses cross product of direction vectors.
Foot of perpendicular: Write parametric point on line, substitute in perpendicular condition, solve for λ.
Line ⊥ plane: Direction ratios of line = coefficients (a,b,c) of plane. Line ∥ plane: a·l+b·m+c·n = 0.
⚡ Ray Optics & Optical Instruments – Physics
Mirror Formula
1/f = 1/v + 1/u
f = R/2 for spherical mirror. Use sign convention carefully.
Mirror Magnification
m = −v/u = h'/h
m > 0 → virtual & erect. m < 0 → real & inverted.
Snell's Law
n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
Also: n = c/v = λ_air/λ_medium. Frequency unchanged.
Critical Angle
sin C = n₂/n₁ = 1/n
TIR occurs when θ > C (denser to rarer medium only).
Apparent Depth
d_app = d_real / n
Object in denser medium, observer in rarer. Normal incidence.
Lens Formula
1/f = 1/v − 1/u
m = v/u. P = 1/f(m). Thin lens formula.
Lens Maker's Equation
1/f = (n−1)[1/R₁ − 1/R₂]
n = refractive index of lens. R₁, R₂ with sign convention.
Combination of Lenses
1/f = 1/f₁ + 1/f₂ | P = P₁+P₂
For lenses in contact. If separated by d: 1/f=1/f₁+1/f₂−d/f₁f₂.
Prism – Min Deviation
n = sin[(A+δₘ)/2] / sin(A/2)
Thin prism: δ = (n−1)A. r₁=r₂=A/2 at minimum deviation.
Refraction at Sph. Surface
n₂/v − n₁/u = (n₂−n₁)/R
Basis for lens maker's equation. Use sign convention.
Simple Microscope
m = 1 + D/f (near point) | m = D/f (infinity)
D = 25 cm (least distance of distinct vision).
Compound Microscope
M = (L/f_o) × (D/f_e)
L = length of tube. f_o < f_e for good magnification.
Astronomical Telescope
M = f_o / f_e | L = f_o + f_e
Normal adjustment (image at infinity). Large f_o, small f_e.
Rayleigh Scattering
I ∝ 1/λ⁴
Blue light scattered most → blue sky. Red least → used in signals.
🔗 Coordination Compounds – Chemistry
Spin-Only Magnetic Moment
μ = √[n(n+2)] BM
n = number of unpaired electrons. 1 unpaired: 1.73 BM. 5 unpaired: 5.92 BM.
EAN Rule (18-electron)
EAN = Z − oxidation state + (2 × CN)
Effective Atomic Number = noble gas configuration for stability.
CFSE – Octahedral (Strong Field)
CFSE = −0.4×(electrons in t₂g)Δo + 0.6×(eg electrons)Δo
t₂g set lower by 0.4Δo; eg set higher by 0.6Δo.
Tetrahedral vs Octahedral Δ
Δt = (4/9) × Δo
Tetrahedral splitting is always smaller → usually high spin. No SFL effect in tetrahedral.
Spectrochemical Series (key)
I⁻ < Br⁻ < Cl⁻ < F⁻ < OH⁻ < H₂O < NH₃ < en < CN⁻ < CO
Weak field → high spin (more unpaired e⁻). Strong field → low spin.
IUPAC Naming Rule
ligands (alphabetical) + metal[OS] | anionic complex = -ate
Neutral ligands: aqua, ammine, carbonyl, nitrosyl. Anionic: chlorido, cyanido, hydroxido.
Oxidation State Calculation
OS of metal + sum of ligand charges = overall charge
For [Fe(CN)₆]⁴⁻: OS(Fe) + 6(−1) = −4 → OS(Fe) = +2.
Stability Constant (Kf)
Kf = [MLn] / [M][L]ⁿ
Higher Kf = more stable complex. Chelation increases Kf significantly (chelate effect).
📐 3 Dimensional Geometry – Mathematics
Direction Cosines
l = a/√(a²+b²+c²), m = b/..., n = c/...
l² + m² + n² = 1 always. Direction ratios (a,b,c) proportional to DCs.
Angle Between Two Lines
cosθ = |a₁a₂+b₁b₂+c₁c₂| / (|b⃗₁||b⃗₂|)
Perpendicular: a₁a₂+b₁b₂+c₁c₂ = 0. Parallel: a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ = c₁/c₂.
Line – Vector Form
r⃗ = a⃗ + λb⃗
a⃗ = position vector of point on line. b⃗ = direction vector. λ ∈ ℝ.
Line – Cartesian Form
(x−x₁)/a = (y−y₁)/b = (z−z₁)/c
Parametric: x = x₁+aλ, y = y₁+bλ, z = z₁+cλ.
Shortest Distance (Skew Lines)
SD = |(a⃗₂−a⃗₁)·(b⃗₁×b⃗₂)| / |b⃗₁×b⃗₂|
If SD = 0, lines are coplanar (may intersect or be parallel).
Coplanarity of Two Lines
(a⃗₂−a⃗₁)·(b⃗₁×b⃗₂) = 0
If satisfied AND b⃗₁ ≠ kb⃗₂, lines are intersecting. Write as 3×3 determinant.
Equation of Plane (Normal Form)
ax + by + cz + d = 0 | r⃗·n⃗ = p
(a,b,c) = normal to plane. Distance from origin = |d|/√(a²+b²+c²).
Distance – Point to Plane
d = |ax₁+by₁+cz₁+d| / √(a²+b²+c²)
Most frequently used formula in 3D geometry questions in KCET.
Angle Between Two Planes
cosθ = |a₁a₂+b₁b₂+c₁c₂| / (|n⃗₁||n⃗₂|)
Perpendicular planes: a₁a₂+b₁b₂+c₁c₂ = 0. Parallel: ratios equal.
Angle – Line & Plane
sinθ = |a·l + b·m + c·n| / (√(a²+b²+c²)·1)
θ is angle with plane (complement of angle with normal). 0 ≤ θ ≤ π/2.
Intercept Form of Plane
x/a + y/b + z/c = 1
Plane makes intercepts a, b, c on x, y, z axes respectively.
Section Formula in 3D
P = (mx₂+nx₁)/(m+n), (my₂+ny₁)/(m+n), (mz₂+nz₁)/(m+n)
Internal division. Midpoint: m = n = 1. External: use negative n.
